Disease Prevention & Treatment · Since 2016

Guru Nanak Rotary Bhagwan Mahaveer Dialysis Centre

Saving lives. Reducing financial burden.

For families battling kidney failure, dialysis is not optional — it is survival, and its recurring cost can crush an already struggling household. The Club helped establish dialysis centres in Secunderabad and Guntur that deliver life-saving treatment affordably, restoring relief, dignity and hope to the patients who need it most.

Locations
Guru Nanak Medical Centre, Secunderabad · St. Joseph Hospital, Guntur
Beneficiaries
Walk-in patients of all ages from economically weaker sections
Partners
Bhagwan Mahaveer Relief Trust · Indian Red Cross · TRF Global Grant · Granules India & VST (CSR)

Impact: 10,000+ dialysis procedures a year · ~₹3 Crore saved annually for beneficiaries · just ₹300 per session at GMC.

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene · Since 1998

Water & Toilets for Girls' Schools

Basic hygiene that keeps girls in school.

Too many girls leave school not for lack of ambition, but for lack of clean water and safe sanitation. The Club installs toilets, handwash stations and RO water plants across schools and colleges, improving attendance, hygiene, dignity and confidence — especially for adolescent girls.

Coverage
Government schools & colleges across Hyderabad and surrounding regions
Beneficiaries
10,000+ students, with a primary focus on girls and orphans
Funding
Multiple CSR grants and philanthropic partners · Project cost over ₹1 Crore

Impact: 30+ RO water plants · 50+ toilets & handwash stations · a measurable drop in absenteeism among school girls.

Community Economic Development

Women's Tailoring & Livelihood Program

Upskilling for financial independence.

A financially independent woman makes a family's foundations stronger. Through structured tailoring training at two venues — Jeedimetla, run by the Club, and Dhoolpet, in association with SETWIN — economically vulnerable women gain employable skills, confidence and a pathway to independent income.

Venues
Jeedimetla (RCHD) · Dhoolpet (with SETWIN)
Format
Structured, multi-month tailoring training
Beneficiaries
Economically vulnerable women across Hyderabad

Impact: Hundreds of women trained — turning skill into dignity, income and self-belief.

Basic Education & Literacy

Udyamini Program

Empowering young women for careers & leadership.

Many bright young women have the talent to succeed but lack career guidance, confidence and exposure to professional environments. Piloted at Government Degree College for Women, Begumpet, Udyamini builds communication, interview readiness and self-belief through interactive sessions, guest talks, role plays and mock interviews — and continues through digital mentoring.

Pilot
Government Degree College for Women, Begumpet
Programme
30+ hours across 24 interactive sessions
Sponsor
Solitaire Group

Impact: A confidence movement targeting 1,000 girls a year, preparing young women for the future.

Disease Prevention & Treatment · Since 2019

Flow Cytometers for Childhood Leukaemia

Precision diagnosis, giving children hope.

For a child with leukaemia, an accurate diagnosis can mean the difference between uncertainty and recovery. The Club equipped Hyderabad's leading cancer institutions with advanced flow cytometers, enabling faster, more precise diagnosis, timely treatment and a higher chance of recovery — while easing the burden on families.

Locations
Basavatarakam Indo-American Cancer Hospital · MNJ Cancer Hospital, Hyderabad
Beneficiaries
500+ leukaemia patients a year, with free diagnostic tests
Project cost
₹60 Lakh initial · ₹19 Lakh upgrade
Partners
TRF Global Grant & International Partner · Solitaire Group (CSR upgrade)

Impact: ~₹60 Lakh saved each year for economically challenged children and adults.

Disease Prevention & Treatment · Since 2018

Rotary Challa Blood Bank

Timely transfusions for the children who need them most.

For children living with thalassaemia, blood transfusions are a lifelong necessity, not an occasional treatment. The Club established a dedicated blood bank and donated a mobile collection van to the Thalassaemia & Sickle Cell Society, ensuring a safe, dependable supply for vulnerable patients.

Location
Thalassaemia & Sickle Cell Society, Rajendra Nagar, Hyderabad
Beneficiaries
Thalassaemia children and economically challenged patients
Project cost
₹80 Lakh · TRF Global Grant & International Partner

Impact: 3,000+ units collected and distributed a year · 500+ units a month via the Blood Van.

DARE — Deccan Assists Rotary Endeavours

Strong Institutions Empowering Local Leadership

Enabling reach to communities everywhere. Not every deserving community has access to resources — DARE helps change that.

DARE is a collaborative model through which the Rotary Club of Hyderabad Deccan supports smaller rural and suburban Rotary clubs with funding, execution expertise and governance support. Together, these partnerships deliver water access, healthcare, education and livelihood solutions across Telangana and Andhra Pradesh.

DARE proves that when strong institutions empower local leadership, impact reaches further.

How it works

RCHD brings financial resources, execution support and governance guidance. The partner club brings local relationships, community knowledge and accountability. Together, they deliver impact that neither could achieve alone.

  • Focus areas: Healthcare · Water & Sanitation · Education · Economic Empowerment · Infrastructure
  • Frequency: at least one project per month, with a different partner club each time
  • Reach: 40+ partner clubs across Telangana & Andhra Pradesh

What makes DARE different

DARE is not a top-down program. It is a collaborative model built on a grassroots philosophy — the conviction that the most durable change is the change a community itself drives, with the support of partners who genuinely care.

Sample projects

  • Construction of water-access steps at a village water body — benefiting 1,000 villagers
  • Distribution of push carts enabling economic independence for street vendors
