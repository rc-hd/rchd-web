About Rotary

A global movement of neighbours, friends and problem-solvers — and a local Trust turning that spirit into measurable change in Hyderabad.

The Rotary Journey

A Global Force for Good Since 1905

What began as a small fellowship gathering in Chicago in 1905 has become one of the world's most respected service movements. Today, Rotary unites more than 1.4 million members across 46,000+ clubs worldwide — leaders, professionals and citizens committed to solving real human challenges.

From eradicating disease and expanding education, to building peace and supporting communities, Rotary transforms local action into global progress. It is non-political, non-religious and united by one enduring principle:

“Service Above Self.”

  • Founders of Rotary International: Paul P. Harris, Gustavus Loehr, Silvester Schiele & Hiram Shorey
  • One of the world's largest humanitarian service organisations
  • Trusted across generations and borders

The Four-Way Test

Of the things we think, say or do

  1. Is it the truth?
  2. Is it fair to all concerned?
  3. Will it build goodwill & better friendships?
  4. Will it be beneficial to all concerned?

Helping Hands of Rotary Trust

Utilising Every Rupee for the Greater Good

HHRT was created to ensure generosity reaches the ground efficiently, transparently and responsibly — directing contributions into carefully managed projects across healthcare, education, welfare and livelihoods.

Why donors trust HHRT

  • CSR & 80G ready — eligible for CSR partnerships and tax benefits under Section 80G
  • Strong compliance systems and transparent accounting
  • Clear reporting and complete documentation
  • Proven execution capability across major projects

During FY 2024–25, HHRT focused its efforts on three areas: healthcare (dialysis, diagnostics, CT-scan access and eye care), skill development (vocational programmes and educational support), and community infrastructure (sanitation for girls, safe drinking water and hygiene awareness).

Transparency

End Polio Now

Helping End One of Humanity's Cruellest Diseases

Rotary chose to fight back. Through one of the largest public-health partnerships in history, Rotary helped immunise more than a billion children worldwide — protecting them from a debilitating disease and reducing polio cases by over 99%. From 1979's first immunisation drive in the Philippines to the launch of the Global Polio Eradication Initiative, Rotary has remained steadfast.

Partners in eradication: Rotary International · WHO · UNICEF · US CDC · National Governments worldwide
